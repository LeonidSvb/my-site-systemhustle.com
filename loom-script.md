# Loom Script — Pre-Call VSL
**Target:** 4–5 min | **Tone:** Direct, no fluff, Hormozi-style | **Slides:** signal-intro.html

---

## SLIDE 1 — The Problem
*(open recording, show slide 1, look into camera first)*

Hey — quick video before our call.

I want to show you something, because almost every recruiter I talk to has the same issue — and most of them don't realize it until I put a number on it.

Here it is:

Your business runs on referrals. And that's fine — it means you're good at what you do. But referrals are unpredictable. Some months are great. Other months, the phone just... doesn't ring. And you can't build a growth plan around something you can't control.

So you try outbound. You call facilities, you monitor job boards, you follow up. And you get there — fifth.

Barton already called. AMN already called. Three other agencies already called. You're not losing because you're worse than them. You're losing because you're late.

---

## SLIDE 2 — The Real Problem (Timing)
*(advance to slide 2)*

Here's what's actually happening.

When a facility has a coverage gap — they don't post a job on day one. They sit with the problem for a week or two. Then they start making calls. Quiet calls. And there's a 2 to 4 week window right there — before anything goes public — where the deal is essentially won.

Whoever gets in during that window sets the frame. They become the trusted agency. Everyone who calls after the job goes live is just competing on price.

You want to be in that window. Right now, you're not. And it's not because you're not working hard enough — it's because you don't have visibility into what's happening before it goes public.

---

## SLIDE 3 — The Cost
*(advance to slide 3)*

Think about what that actually costs you.

By the time a facility submits a request or posts on a job board — four to six agencies have already been in contact. The first one to call set the expectation. The second one maybe got a second look. You're showing up as number five, competing on margin, hoping they give you a shot.

And it's happening every single week. There are facilities in your market right now — today — that are in pain. They need coverage. They haven't called anyone yet. You just don't know who they are.

That's the gap. That's what I fix.

---

## SLIDE 4 — The Signal Layer
*(advance to slide 4)*

I monitor a layer of data that most agencies don't watch.

Not job boards. Not LinkedIn job posts. The stuff that happens before any of that — leadership changes, regulatory filings, license applications, trade press signals. The kind of data that tells you a facility is about to need coverage three to six weeks before they pick up the phone.

I take those signals, I stack them by urgency, I score them — and then I introduce you to the right facility at exactly the right moment.

Not as a cold vendor. As a referral. They already know your name before you call. They're already expecting to hear from you.

That changes everything about the conversation. You stop being the fifth agency that called this week. You start being the consultant who already understood their situation before the call.

---

## SLIDE 5 — The Call
*(advance to slide 5)*

So here's what I want to do on our call.

I'm going to show you the live signal report for your specific niche — real facilities, real signals, scored by urgency, specific to your market. Not generic data. Your market, right now.

Then we'll figure out together whether this is a fit. No pressure. Forty minutes — you'll know by the end whether this makes sense for you or not.

*(pause, look at camera)*

That's it. See you on the call.

— Leonid

---

## DELIVERY NOTES

- **Pace:** slower than you think. Pause after each key statement. Let it land.
- **Slide 2 timeline visual:** when you say "2 to 4 week window" — point at the highlighted middle block on screen
- **Slide 3 big numbers:** read them out loud — "four to six agencies... two to four weeks... first"
- **Slide 4:** don't rush through the signal types. Each one is a mini-proof point.
- **Slide 5:** slow down at the end. The pause before "See you on the call" does the work.
- **Total runtime target:** 4:00–4:30. If you're under 3:30, you're rushing. Record twice.
